Our Story

A choral society of two voices — one in Arabic, one in the world's repertoire — sharing the same rehearsal room since {{ site.founded_year }}.

{{ site.brand_en }} was founded by a small circle of singers and a single conviction: that the human voice, patient and disciplined, remains the most direct expression of community. We perform in concert halls, churches, and gardens; commission new work from Arab composers; and open our doors twice a year to new voices.

Today, two ensembles share our home — Gardenia, a mixed choir of twenty-four voices working in the great choral tradition, and Tanaghom, a chamber choir of sixteen specializing in Arabic art-song and sacred repertoire.
