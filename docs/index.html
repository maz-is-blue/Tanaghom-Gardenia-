Our Story

A choral society of two voices — one in Arabic, one in the world's repertoire — sharing the same rehearsal room since 2016.

Tanaghom Gardenia was founded by a small circle of singers and a single conviction: that the human voice, patient and disciplined, remains the most direct expression of community. We perform in concert halls, churches, and gardens; commission new work from Arab composers; and open our doors twice a year to new voices.

Today, two ensembles share our home — Gardenia, a mixed choir of twenty-four voices working in the great choral tradition, and Tanaghom, a chamber choir of sixteen specializing in Arabic art-song and sacred repertoire.

Gardenia غاردينيا

A professional all-female choir founded in Damascus in 2016. Combining traditional Syrian melodies with original compositions, Gardenia uses music as a tool for healing, solidarity, and women's empowerment. Winner of Best Women's Choir at the Dubai Choir Festival 2019.

Tanaghom تناغم

A Syrian family of 250+ young men and women from all Syrian governorates. Founded in 2019 with UNDP support, Tanaghom uses music and choral singing to build social cohesion and advocate for human rights. Their album 'My Voice and Your Voice' was entirely composed by its members.
